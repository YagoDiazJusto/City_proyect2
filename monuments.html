Korean Bell of Friendship

The Korean Bell of Friendship (more commonly called Korean Friendship Bell) is a massive bronze bell housed in a stone pavilion in Angel's Gate Park, in the San Pedro neighborhood of Los Angeles, California. Located at the corner of Gaffey and 37th Streets, the section of the park is alternatively called the "Korean–American Peace Park" and occupies part of the former Upper Reservation of Fort MacArthur.

Watts Tower

The Watts Towers, Torres de Simon Rodia, or Nuestro Pueblo are a collection of 17 interconnected towers, architecturally structured, and individual sculptures featuring mosaics on the site where the artist originally resided in Watts, Los Angeles. The entire site of the towers, structures, sculptures, pavement and walls were designed and built by Sabato ("Simon") Rodia, an Italian immigrant and construction worker and tile maker, over a period of 33 years between 1921 to 1954. The height of the towers is 99.5 feet (30.3 m). The towers are an example of outsider art (or Art Brut) and Italian-American naïve art.

Astronomers Monument

Greeting visitors upon their arrival at Griffith Observatory, the Astronomers Monument is a large outdoor concrete sculpture on the front lawn that pays homage to six of the greatest astronomers of all time. The monument is an enduring product of the great economic depression of the 1930s, when New Deal initiatives created federally-funded work programs to employ skilled workers at a time when they would otherwise remain idle and without income. One of the first of these programs, the Public Works of Art Project (PWAP), began in December 1933.
