On this page we will find content about the history of Los Angeles

Los Angeles was founded on September 4, 1781 by 44 Spaniards with the official name of El Pueblo de la Reina de los Ángeles, a name that would be amplified as El Pueblo de Nuestra Señora la Reina de los Ángeles de Porciúncula, by the name that the explorer and missionary Juan Crespí gave the Los Angeles River in Gaspar de Portolá's expedition in 1769.

The 1994 Northridge Earthquake killed nearly a hundred people and severely damaged the city's complex highway system. This entire region is shaken by seismic movements regularly, because it is located on the so-called "San Andreas fault".
