Los Angeles, as we already know, belongs to a state of the USA, California.

California is the name that Spanish conquerors assigned to an area of ​​America corresponding to the peninsula that today bears that same name and to a region located to the north of it. This name comes from an imaginary place mentioned in Las sergas de Esplandián, a novel of chivalry by Garci Rodríguez de Montalvo, published in 1510. It is still disputed where exactly the idea of ​​using such a name came from, to what was initially believed to be a island and was called California.

Los Angeles

The name of Los Angeles> derives from Santa Maria degli Angeli ("Holy Mary of the Angels"), the Italian town that houses the Porziuncola, the chapel where the Franciscan movement originated. Friar Crespí, Junípero Serra, and the early California missionaries did indeed belong to the Franciscan Order.
